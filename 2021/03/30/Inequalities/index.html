Inequalities

Posted on 2021-03-30 | In math

\(f(x)\),\(g(x)\) integrable on \([a,b]\)

Schwarz

\[[\int_a^bf(x)g(x)dx]^2\leq\int_a^bf^2(x)dx\cdot\int_a^bg^2(x)dx\]

Minkowski

\[\{\int_a^b[f(x)+g(x)]^2dx\}^\frac{1}{2}\leq\{\int_a^bf^2(x)dx\}^\frac{1}{2}+\{\int_a^bg^2(x)dx\}^\frac{1}{2}\]

Chebychev

\[P(x>t)\leq\frac{\sigma^2}{\sigma^2+(t-\mu)^2}\]

The Classic Approximation

\[\int_\Omega f(x)g(x)dx\approx\frac{\int_\Omega f(x)dx}{\int_\Omega dx}\cdot\int_\Omega g(x)dx\] when is accurate? - g(x)'s support is small - g(x) is smooth

light precompute (IBL)

\[L_o(p,\omega_o)\approx\frac{\int_{\Omega_{f_r}} L_i(p,\omega_i)d\omega_i}{\int_{\Omega_{f_r}} d\omega_i}\cdot\int_{\Omega^+} f_r(p,\omega_i,\omega_o)cos\theta_i d\omega_i\]

shadow

\[L_o(p,\omega_o)\approx\frac{\int_{\Omega^+} V(p,\omega_i)d\omega_i}{\int_{\Omega^+} d\omega_i}\cdot\int_{\Omega^+} L_i(p,\omega_i)f_r(p,\omega_i,\omega_o)cos\theta_i d\omega_i\]

(split sum) integral -> sum

\[\frac{1}{N}\sum_{k=1}^N\frac{L_i(l_k)f(l_k,v)cos\theta_{l_k}}{p(l_k,v)}\approx (\frac{1}{N}\sum_{k=1}^NL_i(l_k))(\frac{1}{N}\sum_{k=1}^N\frac{f(l_k,v)cos\theta_{l_k}}{p(l_k,v)})\]
